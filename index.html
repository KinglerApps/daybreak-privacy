Privacy Policy for Daybreak

Effective date: June 1, 2026

Daybreak ("the app," "we," "us") is a focus and screen-time app. This policy explains what data the app accesses, why, and how it is handled. Our core principle is that your personal usage data stays on your device, with the limited exceptions described below for crash reporting and advertising.

Data We Collect

Daybreak does not have user accounts, and it does not collect, store, or upload your app-usage history, blocking schedules, or settings to any server. That information remains on your device. The exceptions are anonymous crash data and advertising data, described in the sections below.

Permissions We Use and Why

  • Usage Access: Daybreak reads which app is currently in the foreground so it can detect when you open an app you've chosen to block during a scheduled window. This is processed entirely on your device and is never transmitted off it.
  • Accessibility Service: Daybreak uses the Accessibility Service to enforce blocking — for example, to display the block/shield screen over a distracting app. It is used solely for this blocking function and does not read, store, or share the content of your screen.
  • Notifications: Daybreak sends notifications to inform you about active blocking windows and related app status. These are generated locally on your device.

Advertising

Daybreak displays advertisements through Google AdMob. To serve ads, AdMob may collect and process certain data, including a device advertising identifier, IP address, and information about your interactions with ads. This data is collected by Google in accordance with its own policies and may be used to provide personalized or non-personalized advertising. You can learn how Google uses this data at https://policies.google.com/technologies/partner-sites, and you can manage your advertising identifier in your device settings.

Crash Reporting

To keep the app stable, Daybreak uses Firebase Crashlytics (a Google service) to collect anonymous crash and diagnostic data when the app stops unexpectedly. This may include the device model, operating system version, and technical details about the crash. It does not include the personal usage data described above, and it is not used to identify you. Firebase Crashlytics is governed by Google's Privacy Policy at https://policies.google.com/privacy.

Data Sharing

We do not sell or rent your personal data. Data is shared only with Google as needed to provide advertising (AdMob) and crash reporting (Firebase Crashlytics), as described above. We do not share your on-device usage history, schedules, or settings with anyone.

Children's Privacy

Daybreak is not directed at children under 13 and does not knowingly collect personal information from them.

Changes to This Policy

We may update this policy from time to time. Any changes will be posted on this page with an updated effective date.
