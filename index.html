Privacy Policy

Daybreak — Focus & Screen Time
Your data stays on your device

Effective date: June 1, 2026

Daybreak (“the app,” “we,” “us”) is a focus and screen-time app. This policy explains what the app accesses, why, and how it’s handled. Our core principle: your personal usage data stays on your device, with the limited exceptions below for advertising and crash reporting.

Key Points

  • 📱Your usage history, schedules, and settings stay on your device — we never upload them.
  • 📢We show ads via Google AdMob, which collects your advertising ID and ad-interaction data.
  • 🛠️We use Firebase Crashlytics to collect anonymous crash data so we can fix bugs.
  • 🚫We never sell or rent your data, and there are no user accounts.

Data We Collect

Daybreak has no user accounts. It does not collect, store, or upload your app-usage history, blocking schedules, or settings to any server — that information stays on your device. The only exceptions are advertising data and anonymous crash data, described below.

Permissions We Use

  • Usage Access Lets Daybreak see which app is currently open, so it can detect when you launch an app you’ve chosen to block during a scheduled window. Processed entirely on-device and never transmitted.
  • Accessibility Service Used to enforce blocking — for example, showing the block/shield screen over a distracting app. Used solely for blocking; it does not read, store, or share your screen content.
  • Notifications Used to inform you about active blocking windows and app status. Generated locally on your device.

Advertising

Daybreak displays ads through Google AdMob. To serve ads, AdMob may collect and process a device advertising identifier, IP address, and information about your interactions with ads. This data is collected by Google under its own policies and may be used for personalized or non-personalized advertising.

Learn how Google uses this data at policies.google.com/technologies/partner-sites. You can reset or limit your advertising identifier in your device settings.

Crash Reporting

Daybreak uses Firebase Crashlytics (a Google service) to collect anonymous crash and diagnostic data when the app stops unexpectedly. This may include device model, OS version, and technical crash details. It does not include the on-device usage data above and is not used to identify you.

Governed by Google’s Privacy Policy at policies.google.com/privacy.

Data Sharing

We do not sell or rent your personal data. Data is shared only with Google as needed to provide advertising (AdMob) and crash reporting (Firebase Crashlytics). We never share your on-device usage history, schedules, or settings with anyone.

Your Rights & Choices

Because Daybreak stores your usage data only on your device, you stay in full control of it:

• Advertising ID: You can reset or delete your advertising identifier, or opt out of personalized ads, in your device’s settings (Settings → Google → Ads, on most Android devices).

• On-device data: Uninstalling Daybreak removes all of its locally stored data from your device.

• Permissions: You can revoke Usage Access, the Accessibility Service, or Notifications at any time in your device settings (note that blocking features depend on them).

• Regional rights: Depending on where you live (for example, under the GDPR or CCPA), you may have rights to access, correct, or delete personal data. Since we hold no personal data on our servers, requests about advertising or crash data should be directed to Google, or you can contact us using the details below.
